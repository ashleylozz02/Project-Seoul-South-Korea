To Do

Gyeongbokgung Palace

The Gyeongbokgung Palace in Seoul, South Korea, was built in 1395 and is the largest of the Five Grand Palaces established by the Joseon Dynasty to serve as the residence of the dynasty's monarchs. It is currently a historical site, a tourist destination, and a museum. Gyeongbokgung Palace's target audience would be teenagers to adults.

Lotte World

Lotte World, comparable to Disney World, is a theme park in Seoul, South Korea, with the world's largest indoor theme park. It first opened its doors in July 1989 and now attracts over 7 million people each year. Lotte World admission costs roughly $50.00 for adults and $40.00 for youngsters. Lotte World's target audience would be youngsters to adults.

Bukhansan National Park

Bukhansan National Park, is also known as Samgaksan because of its three peaks: Baegunbong, Insubong, and Mangyeongbong. It is the only national park in the Seoul metropolitan area. The park's numerous granite peaks and gorges are punctuated by crystal clear streams, resulting in a wonderful balance of mountains and water.

Myeong-dong

Myeongdong is Seoul's shopping hotspot and a must-see for travelers, but it is more than simply a shopping destination; with a daytime population of 1.5-2 million, Myeongdong is also a hub of trade, banking, and culture.
